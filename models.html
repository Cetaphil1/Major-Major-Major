Most online major quizzes use one signal: what you like. We compare that baseline against a deeper model that reads eight signals — and surfaces the students who like their major but are quietly at risk of switching.

02From answers to a read

How a switch-risk read is built.

Your answers become eight dimension scores, which combine into an overall fit, a switching-risk level, and a plain-language explanation of what's driving it.

01 · Score each signalShort statements are averaged into a 0–100 score per dimension, with reverse-worded items flipped.
02 · Weigh the tensionHigh interest against low workload, belonging, or burnout is the pattern that predicts quiet switching.
03 · Estimate the riskSwitching and burnout risk are leveled from the operational signals and your stated intent.
04 · Explain the whyThe report names whether it's the major, the school, the workload, the career, or outside pressure.

This prototype computes a transparent average-and-weight pass over your answers — not a clinical or validated instrument. It's meant to start an honest conversation, ideally with an academic advisor.
