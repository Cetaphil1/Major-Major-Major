Similar Majors as Safer Comparisons


Switching majors does not have to mean leaping into the unknown. Comparing your current major to closely related ones is a lower-risk way to test whether a change would actually help.


Similar Majors as Safer Comparisons


  • Adjacent majors: Fields that share courses, skills, or careers with your current one.

  • Why they are safer: You keep more credits, build on what you know, and change less at once.

  • What to compare: Workload, motivation, belonging, and career clarity side by side.


How the Quiz Helps


  • Shared ground: Highlights what an adjacent major keeps from your current path.

  • Real differences: Shows where workload or career clarity actually improves.

  • Honest trade-offs: Names what you would give up, not just what you would gain.


A Quick Example


A student unhappy in one major compares it to a neighbor that shares half its courses. Career clarity jumps, workload stays manageable, and little progress is lost. That is a switch worth considering.


Looking Ahead


Do not compare your major to a fantasy — compare it to real, reachable alternatives. Adjacent majors give you a fair, low-cost test of whether switching pays off.


The Takeaway

.

The safest switch is often a small one. Use the quiz to compare similar majors before assuming a dramatic change is the only option.
