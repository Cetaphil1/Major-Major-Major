02 Why students really leave

It's rarely just "too hard."

Switching and dropping out usually trace to something more specific. We measure the real reasons — not just whether you enjoy the subject.

Workload & burnoutThe pace and volume grind you down faster than the interest can carry you.
Financial pressureCost, hours worked, and time-to-degree quietly force a switch.
Family & status pressureA major chosen for safety, prestige, or someone else's plan.
Belonging & isolationFeeling like an outsider in the field — one of the strongest predictors of leaving.
Low academic confidenceFeeling behind your peers, even when you're capable.
Unclear career pathYou can't picture — or don't want — where the degree leads.
Poor school fitIt's the program or campus culture, not the field itself.
Feeling trappedStaying in a major you don't actually value because leaving feels too costly.
